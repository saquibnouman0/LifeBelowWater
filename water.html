Water is Life

    Water conservation is Essential

    Just because some people have easy access to water, we are forgetting and are losing sight that water conservation is very crucial.

    Water is essential for flora and fauna

    The main difference between earth and other planets is presence of water. No any other planet has so much content of water as in ours. Water is essential for the life of all flora and fauna on Earth, which is why it must flow continuously.

    Access to water is crucial

    Access to safe water is crucial to sustainable development. Climate change and population growth are putting increasing pressure on available water resources, FAO (or Food and Agriculture Organization) is urging countries to make efforts to increase water efficiency and provide access to safe water for all.

    We have less freshwater left

    With 16% of the world population, India only has 4% of the total available freshwater. Agriculture only accounts for 80% of India's total water consumption. We have less freshwater left with us.

    “Namami Gange”

    The biggest water rejuvenation plan launched by the Indian government is “Namami Gange” for the Ganges river basin.

    Most of population of India do not have acess to piped water

    The water prerequisite of India is 1100 billion cubic meters per year. 84% of rural households do not have access to piped water lines.

    Ice and glaciers contain freshwater

    Around 10 crore people consume water with an excessive amount of fluoride. Almost 68% of the earth's fresh water is contained in Ice and glaciers. Freshwater can be found in rivers, streams, and lakes and also in glaciers and groundwater.

    Conserve or Die

    These facts tell more than enough about why conservation of water is important. The current situation regarding the depleting amount of water tells us about the need and importance of why it is essential to conserve water.
